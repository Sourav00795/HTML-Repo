READING GLASSES

Reading glasses, also known as readers, are eyeglasses designed to improve near vision for individuals with presbyopia, a common age-related condition. Presbyopia typically occurs around the age of 40 and is characterized by a gradual loss of the ability to focus on close-up objects.
Reading glasses contain lenses that have a specific optical power, measured in diopters, which helps compensate for the reduced ability to focus on nearby objects. These lenses are convex (thicker in the middle and thinner at the edges) and have a positive power. The lens power required for reading glasses can vary depending on the individual's specific vision needs.



COMPUTER GLASSES


Computer glasses, also known as blue light glasses or computer eyewear, are specially designed glasses that aim to reduce eye strain and discomfort caused by prolonged computer use. These glasses are designed to filter out or block blue light emitted by digital screens, such as computer monitors, smartphones, and tablets.



SUNGLASSES

Sunglasses are a type of eyewear specifically designed to protect the eyes from the harmful effects of the sun's ultraviolet (UV) rays and intense light. In addition to providing eye protection, sunglasses also serve as a fashion accessory and can enhance visual comfort in bright environments.
